A Rasberry Pi NES Emulator

Notes for the RasPi Nes project

Download this project as a .zip file Download this project as a tar.gz file

I have been waiting for a low power computer to stick in an old console to relive the 1980's in all of it's 8 bit glory.

Parts List

  • Broken NES found at flea market (Just ask the VG vendors)
  • 1 200 ohm resistor (I desoldered one from the broken NES)
  • 2 USB extension cables
  • 1 Flush mount ethernet or similar (monoprice.com)
  • 1 1/8" audio jack 6" extension ( I made my own for US $2)
  • 1 breadboard/prototype board or planar board if you want it finished looking
  • 1 short HDMI or HDMI extension cable (monoprice.com)
  • 1 Any power adapter that meets spec. (the male end doesn't matter)
  • 1 micro USB end. (monoprice or cut up a usb cable)
  • 2 USB NES controllers
  • 1 small block of craft foam (I shaped and hardened it for the Pi mount)
  • x Miscellaneous Wires, Caps, cable ties.

Software:

Raspian RetroPi-Setup for (retro pi, libretro, emulationstation, retroarch)
