A Rasberry Pi NES Emulator

Notes for the RasPi Nes project

I have been waiting for a low power computer to stick in an old console to relive the 1980's in all of it's 8 bit glory. My objective was to make the case appear relatively stock but I have made some exceptions. I also wanted the unit to function in a reasonable way via the power, reset and joypad only. I had started the project thinking I would have to do most of the programming myself beyond the libretro implementation I was going to use but fortunately there are some really great projects out there already doing this. RetroArch, RetroPi, EmulationStation and RetroPie-Setup to bring it all together. I am hoping to contribute to all of them now that I'm at the software part of my plans but they've all worked so well thus far that I haven't had much need.

Parts List

  • Broken NES found at flea market (Just ask the VG vendors)
  • 1 200 ohm resistor (I desoldered one from the broken NES)
  • 2 USB extension cables
  • 1 Flush mount ethernet or similar (monoprice.com)
  • 1 1/8" audio jack 6" extension ( I made my own for US $2)
  • 1 breadboard/prototype board or planar board if you want it finished looking
  • 1 short HDMI or HDMI extension cable (monoprice.com)
  • 1 Any power adapter that meets spec. (the male end doesn't matter)
  • 1 micro USB end. (monoprice or cut up a usb cable)
  • 2 USB NES controllers
  • 1 small block of craft foam (I shaped and hardened it for the Pi mount)
  • x Miscellaneous Wires, Caps, cable ties.

Software:

  • Raspian
  • RetroPi-Setup for (retro pi, libretro, emulationstation, retroarch)
  • Some Roms to Play
